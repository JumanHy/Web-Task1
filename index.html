Dell Inc., headquartered in Round Rock, Texas, is a global technology company that designs, develops, manufactures, and supports personal computers (PCs) and a variety of computer-related products. Founded in 1984 by American entrepreneur Michael Dell, the company initially operated under the name PC’s Limited. Michael Dell started the business from his dormitory room at the University of Texas in Austin, providing customized upgrades for PCs.

Dell’s unique approach involved creating and selling custom-built PCs directly to consumers, allowing them to offer high-quality PCs at competitive prices. The company emphasized customer support and implemented a policy of risk-free returns. Over the years, Dell expanded its product line to include televisions, digital cameras, and other computer-related products. In 2003, the company was renamed Dell Inc., signifying its move into the broader consumer electronics market.
